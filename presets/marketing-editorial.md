# marketing / editorial

## best for

landing pages, storytelling pages, launches, brand surfaces, case studies, product narratives, campaigns.

use this when mood, identity, and point of view matter as much as correctness.

## activates

- `design-review`
- `ux-baseline-check`
- `ui-polish-pass`
- `agent-friendly-design` when shipping to production
- `responsive.md`
- `ai-css-failure-patterns.md`
- `whimsical-design` when the brief wants personality or delight
- `world-build` when the brief explicitly wants atmosphere or immersion
- `web-animation-design` when motion is part of the concept

## intentionally skips

- dense utilitarian defaults as the main aesthetic
- dashboard-style composition unless the brief truly needs it

## design posture

- identity-forward
- atmosphere matters
- rhythm, pacing, and composition matter as much as correctness
- responsive behavior should preserve the story, not just the layout

## quality bar

- the page has a point of view
- sections have varied rhythm and visual weight
- motion, if present, feels purposeful and interruptible
- mobile gets its own thoughtful translation when needed
- the work feels designed, not merely assembled

## recommended inputs

- visual references or brand cues
- desired emotional register
- hero message and conversion goal
- asset direction: imagery, illustration, motion, typography
- `DESIGN.md` or project identity notes with atmosphere language

## human judgment still required

- whether the concept is actually distinctive or just louder
- when motion improves the story vs distracts from it
- how far to push personality before usability suffers
- what should change completely on mobile to preserve impact

## run report defaults

when this preset is active, the run report should usually emphasize:

- **task classification:** marketing page / editorial surface / storytelling UI / launch page
- **skills fired:** `design-review`, `ux-baseline-check`, `ui-polish-pass`, plus `whimsical-design`, `world-build`, or `web-animation-design` only when the brief earns them
- **skills skipped:** dense utilitarian defaults as aesthetic guidance unless the brief mixes modes
- **references consulted:** `spacing.md`, `typography.md`, `anti-patterns.md`, `responsive.md`, `ai-css-failure-patterns.md`, plus creative references as needed
- **issues detected:** generic saas tropes, weak visual rhythm, atmosphere collapse on mobile, decorative motion without purpose, loudness without point of view
- **fixes applied:** stronger composition, clearer atmosphere, better section pacing, more intentional responsive translation, tighter motion use
- **human judgment required:** whether the concept has a real point of view and how far to push expression

## example prompt framing

- build a page with a strong editorial point of view
- treat spacing and rhythm as part of the message
- make the mobile version preserve the emotional arc, not just the blocks
- avoid generic saas gradients and stock launch-page tropes
