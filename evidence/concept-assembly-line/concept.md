# Concept: assembly line

## Aesthetic thesis

A playful cutaway workshop: one real Orders/admin screen rides a continuous mechanical track through five inspection stations, with each stop making a visible change to the artifact before a final release gate.

## World brief

- **World:** a daylight industrial design workshop that becomes a deep-blue night shift in dark mode.
- **Metaphor:** ADS is the inspection line around the builder, not an automatic design generator.
- **Emotion:** confident curiosity. The process should feel inspectable, physical, and a little delightful.

## Reference contract

- **Source/reference:** production baseline at `memory/artifacts/agentic-design-system/vnext-2026-07-14/production-main-1581ad7/default-1280x800.png`; existing `creative-pipeline-light.png` and `creative-pipeline-dark.png`; Playdate product storytelling, illustrated technical cutaways, Swiss industrial manuals, and Teenage Engineering functional labels as same-spirit references.
- **Primary borrowed layer:** illustrated workshop world, tactile cream/orange materials, deep-blue night mode, oversized type, and the 01-05 teaching spine.
- **Secondary borrowed layers:** measurement marks, inspection lamps, stamped failures, rails, release hardware, and small functional labels.
- **What not to borrow:** generic SaaS card grids, dashboard bento layouts, tiny screenshots, beige-on-beige repetition, receipt tables as hero content, glass chrome, ornamental gauges, or the rejected PR #23 / rejected vNext compositions.
- **Fidelity target:** same product world, structurally freer.
- **Product constraints:** one Orders artifact remains legible at every stop; transformation leads and verdict/receipt stays subordinate; install and GitHub actions remain obvious; semantic HTML, visible focus, reduced motion, and responsive behavior are required.
- **Success cues:** the page reads as one continuous mechanism; each station visibly alters the same object; 01-05 can be understood without explanatory prose; light and dark feel like day and night shifts.
- **Failure cues:** five repeated cards, a generic hero plus screenshots, decorative factory motifs without meaning, or an audit report masquerading as a homepage.

## Baseline critique

The production page has a strong illustrated hero and warm identity, but below the fold it falls back to centered headings and repeated beige panels. The product loop is explained mainly with text and receipts, so the page asks the viewer to trust its design claims instead of watching design quality change an artifact. This concept keeps the successful world and teaching spine while turning the proof into a large, continuous visual story.
